Brief

Cake cutting is a common tradition at many celebrations, including weddings, birthdays, and anniversaries. It is typically done as a symbolic gesture to mark a special occasion and is often accompanied by a toast or other expressions of good wishes. This app target customers who need to order cake immediately and delivery the cake on their doorstep with scheduling. View the prototype here.

Problem

Busy workers and commuters who needs to celebrate events by cake cutting and who forget order cake in special events.

Goals

1. Design a Cake ordering app that allows users to easily order cakes
2. Orders must deliver immediately and deliver on speciffic date and time.
3. UI must be simple as accessible.

User Research

I conducted interviews and created empathy maps to understand the users I'm designing for and their needs. A primary user group identified through research was working adults who need cake for immediate celebrations on their doorstep. This user group confirmed initial assumptions about Cake Factory App, but research also revealed that time was not the only factor limiting users who needs to order immediately. Other user problems included physically abled who can't go outside, Persons who don't know how to handle cake, who needs to surprise even they are distance.

Pain points

1. Working Adults and Students who often think about cake when involved in sudden celebration.
2. People who orders special Big Layering cakes. But they don't know how to handle without Damage.
3. People who need to order cake before and scheduling their orders on right Day and Time.

Personas

Prakash is a Student who needs to order cake on Sudden Celebration and deliver on require Location. So they can make surprise others and enjoy.

User journey map

Mapping Prakash's user journey revealed how helpful it would be for users to have access to order cake from the Cake Factory app.

Paper wireframe

Taking the time to draft iterations of each screen of the app on paper ensured that the elements that made it to digital wireframes would be well-suited to address user pain points. For the home screen, I prioritized a quick and easy ordering process to help users save time.

Digital wireframe

As the initial design phase continues, I made sure to base screen designs on feedback and findings from the user research.

Low-ﬁdelity prototype

Using the completed set of digital wireframes, I created a low-ﬁdelity prototype. The primary user ﬂow I connected was building and ordering a Cake, so the prototype could be used in a usability study.

Usability study: ﬁndings

1. User want to add name on cake
2. Don't need two pages for ingredients and review.
3. Need sidebar for better navigation.

Mockups

In Early designs, I set every options in sidebar. After usability study. I designed Bottom bars and separated the options for easy navigation.

The second usability study revealed the frustration of navigating Reviews and Ingredients section. I Designed Ingridents are vertically aligned and Reviews are Horizontally aligned.
