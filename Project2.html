Brief

Cryptocurrency offers a number of benefits, including the ability to securely and transparently make and receive payments, as well as the ability to store and transfer value without the need for a central authority. Customers that need to swap cryptocurrency in a decentralised (DEX) way are the target audience for this app. View the prototype here.

Why I Choose this project.

Cryptocurrencies are now a big topic in modern world, with some claiming they will eventually replace money. I thus choose to participate in this technology. Cryptos have greater power when used decentralizedly. DEXs, or decentralised exchanges, are peer-to-peer markets where cryptocurrency traders may conduct transactions without entrusting the administration of their assets to a middleman or custodian.

Smart contracts, which are self-executing contracts written in computer code, are used to expedite these transactions. DEXs were developed to do away with the need for any authority to monitor and approve deals executed within a particular exchange.

Problem

How might we make the user interface easier to use so that users may exchange their cryptocurrencies in a decentralised exchange wallet? DEXs can encourage more user participation.

Goals

1. Allow people to keep their cryptocurrency coins.
2. Users may easily check the market data's past performance, learn more about it, and follow a coin's transactions..
3. Options for importing an old wallet into a new device.

User Research

I first sent a survey to three user groups: experienced cryptocurrency traders, frequent wallet users, and cryptocurrency enthusiasts who aren't totally immersed in the currency. Find out whether they are exchanging cryptocurrency on CEX or DEX. There are issues with the apps they use.

Insights

1. Users are dissatisfied with the existing backup method in other apps.
2. Due to backup problems, users have lost access to their wallets.
3. DEXs are not user-friendly for beginners and are better suited for traders who have experience in the cryptocurrency market due to their lack of intuitiveness.
4. The majority of DEX wallets don't offer a simple way to monitor transactions or activity.
5. In order to keep their assets separate from their families, customers choose to have multiple wallets within a single app. They may also utilise it like bank applications by having many accounts for various purposes.
